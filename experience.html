Research Experience

  • Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) (Summer 2019)

    SLANG Lab at UMass, Amherst, MA

    During the summer of 2019, I work as an undergraduate research assistant at SLANG Lab at UMass under the mentorship of Professor Brendan O'Connor and PhD Student Katherine Keith. My work focuses on the problem of prevalence estimation in unlabeled data in low-resource settings, and is an extension of Prof.O'Connor and K.Keith's Uncertainty-aware generative models for inferring document class prevalence. Within my project, I build deep learning sentiment classifiers using Deep Averaging Network (DAN) architecture. We want to identify if there is a linear relationship between the classifiers' performances in the task sentiment classification (measured by accuracy and cross-entropy loss) and their performances in the task of prevalence estimation (measured by mean absolute error). We also want to identify important hyper-parameters of DAN in order to build efficient real-world prevalence estimators.
